Poster Competition

Poster Competition Guidelines

Overview

The Top Three Poster Competition Awards recognize outstanding poster presentations during the Lunch and Poster Session at the Joint Biostatistics Symposium. Each winner will receive a certificate and a gift card.

Eligibility

While all researchers—including faculty, professionals, students, and trainees—are encouraged to submit and present posters, only students and postdoctoral researchers are eligible for the poster competition awards this year.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Presentation Requirement: Participants must present their research during the Poster Session on Monday, April 7th, 2025.
  • Research Focus: Submissions should feature either:
    • Innovative quantitative methodologies
    • Creative applications of statistical analyses pertinent to biometric applications
  • Authorship: The participant must be the first author. Co-authorship with faculty advisors or a limited number of collaborators is permitted.

Submission Guidelines

  • Abstract Submission Deadline: All abstracts must be submitted by 11:59 p.m. ET on March 12, 2025.
  • Submission Portal: Submit your abstract through the online form.
  • Competition Participation: Indicate your interest in the Poster Competition by selecting “YES” in the relevant section of the submission form.

Note: Participants are required to register for the symposium prior to submission. Register here.

Review and Judging Criteria

Award winners will be selected based on an on-site evaluation during the Lunch and Poster Session.

Session Details:

  • Date and Time: Monday, April 7, 2025, from 11:30 a.m. to 1:00 p.m.
  • Presenter Availability: Presenters should be stationed by their posters from 12:00 p.m. to 1:00 p.m.

Evaluation Criteria:

  1. Visual Presentation: Clarity and organization of the poster, including clear section headings and legible content.
  2. Verbal Presentation: Ability to effectively communicate the research problem, methodology, and findings.
  3. Methodological Rigor: Strength and innovation of the methodology and its relevance to the research objectives.

Judges will circulate anonymously during the session and assess posters based on the criteria above.

Poster Preparation Tips

  • Design and Layout:
    • Title and Headings: Use clear and concise titles with section headings to guide viewers.
    • Font Size: Ensure all text is legible from a distance of at least 3 feet.
    • Visuals: Incorporate high-quality graphics, charts, and images to complement the text.
    • White Space: Balance text and visuals with adequate white space to enhance readability.
  • Content Organization:
    • Introduction: Provide background and state the research question or hypothesis.
    • Methods: Summarize the methodology concisely.
    • Results: Highlight key findings with visual aids.
    • Conclusion: Discuss the implications and potential future directions.

For detailed guidance on creating effective posters, refer to resources such as the Guide to Effective Poster Design.

By adhering to these guidelines, participants can ensure a smooth submission process and enhance their chances of success in the Poster Competition.
